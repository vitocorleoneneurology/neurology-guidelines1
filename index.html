AHA/ASA 2026
Acute Ischemic Stroke
LAST UPDATED: JAN 2026 · Class I–IIa recommendations
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
Hyperacute Algorithm
1
tPA (Alteplase) window
0–4.5 h from symptom onset. Dose 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg); 10% IV bolus, remainder over 60 min.
Exclusions: BP >185/110 · INR >1.7
Prior ICH · Major surgery <14d
2
Tenecteplase (TNK)
Class IIa: 0.25 mg/kg IV single bolus, 0–4.5 h. Preferred prior to thrombectomy.
3
Mechanical Thrombectomy
Large vessel occlusion, NIHSS ≥6, ASPECTS ≥6. Door-to-puncture <90 min.
Window: 0–24 h with imaging selection
DAWN / DEFUSE-3 criteria beyond 6 h
4
Blood Pressure Targets
Pre-tPA: reduce to <185/110 before giving. Post-tPA: maintain <180/105 for 24 h. No thrombolytics: allow up to 220/120 (Class IIb).
Secondary Prevention
Antiplatelet (aspirin 81–325 mg) within 24–48 h if no thrombolytics.
Dual antiplatelet (aspirin + clopidogrel) × 21 days for minor stroke or high-risk TIA (NIHSS ≤3, ABCD2 ≥4).
AF-related stroke: start anticoagulation 4–14 days post-stroke.
Statin therapy regardless of baseline LDL. Target LDL <70 mg/dL.
Symptomatic carotid stenosis ≥70%: CEA within 2 weeks.
⚠ BP Pitfall
Permissive hypertension (up to 220/120) is only for non-thrombolysis candidates. Aggressive lowering increases infarct extension risk in first 24 h.
AHA/ASA 2022
Intracerebral Hemorrhage
SPONTANEOUS ICH · BP · REVERSAL · SURGERY
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
Acute BP Management
1
SBP 150–220 mmHg
Acute lowering to SBP <140 mmHg is safe and may improve functional outcome (Class IIa). Target within 1 hour of presentation.
Nicardipine IV infusion (preferred)
OR Labetalol IV bolus/infusion
OR Clevidipine IV infusion
2
SBP >220 mmHg
Aggressive reduction with continuous IV infusion and close monitoring. Target SBP <140 mmHg.
Anticoagulation Reversal
1
Warfarin (elevated INR)
4-Factor PCC (Kcentra) — preferred over FFP
+ Vitamin K 10 mg IV
Target INR <1.5 urgently
2
Dabigatran
Idarucizumab (Praxbind) 5 g IV (2 × 2.5 g)
3
Factor Xa inhibitors (apixaban, rivaroxaban)
Andexanet alfa (AndexXa) — FDA approved
OR 4-Factor PCC if andexanet unavailable
4
Antiplatelet-associated ICH
Platelet transfusion NOT recommended routinely (PATCH trial: harm). Use only if emergency surgery planned.
Surgical Considerations
1
Cerebellar ICH
Surgical evacuation if diameter >3 cm OR neurological deterioration OR brainstem compression. Do not delay.
2
Supratentorial ICH
Minimally invasive surgery (MIS) — Class IIb for selected patients with superficial lobar hematoma. Standard craniotomy not routinely superior to medical management (STICH trials).
3
Hydrocephalus / IVH
EVD placement for hydrocephalus with decreased consciousness. Intraventricular tPA (alteplase) to accelerate clot lysis (CLEAR III trial).
ICH Score — Prognosis
GCS 3–4 = 2 pts · GCS 5–12 = 1 pt · GCS 13–15 = 0 pts
ICH volume ≥30 mL = 1 pt · Infratentorial = 1 pt · IVH present = 1 pt · Age ≥80 = 1 pt
Score 0–1: low mortality. Score 4–6: very high 30-day mortality.
⚠ Hematoma Expansion
30% of ICH patients expand in first 24 h. Spot sign on CTA predicts expansion. Aggressive SBP control to <140 mmHg within 1 hour is the key modifiable factor.
AHA/ASA 2023
Subarachnoid Hemorrhage
ANEURYSMAL SAH · VASOSPASM · COMPLICATIONS
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
Grading & Initial Management
1
Hunt-Hess & WFNS Grading
Hunt-Hess I: mild HA, no neuro deficit
Hunt-Hess II: severe HA, nuchal rigidity
Hunt-Hess III: drowsy, mild deficit
Hunt-Hess IV–V: stupor/coma
2
BP Control Pre-Securing
Maintain SBP <160 mmHg until aneurysm secured to prevent rebleeding. Nicardipine or labetalol IV.
3
Aneurysm Securing
Endovascular coiling preferred over surgical clipping when both feasible (ISAT trial). Clip for MCA aneurysms, young patients, or large hematoma needing evacuation.
Target: within 24–72 h of presentation
Vasospasm Prevention & Treatment
1
Nimodipine (mandatory)
60 mg PO/NG q4h × 21 days
Reduces poor neurological outcome (Class I)
Does NOT prevent angiographic vasospasm
2
Euvolemia
Maintain normovolemia with isotonic fluids. Prophylactic hypervolemia (HHH therapy) is no longer recommended — increased complications without benefit.
3
Delayed Cerebral Ischemia (DCI)
Induced hypertension (SBP target 160–200 mmHg) for clinical vasospasm. Intra-arterial verapamil or balloon angioplasty for refractory cases.
SAH Complications to Monitor
Hydrocephalus (20%): EVD if acute. VP shunt if chronic.
Rebleeding: highest risk in first 24 h (4–13%). Secure aneurysm urgently.
Hyponatremia: cerebral salt wasting (give Na) vs SIADH (restrict fluids). Fludrocortisone for CSW.
Seizures: levetiracetam prophylaxis short-term (Class IIb); avoid phenytoin.
Cardiac: neurogenic stunned myocardium, Takotsubo pattern — troponin + Echo in all.
AHA/ASA 2021
TIA & Minor Stroke
RAPID WORKUP · ABCD2 · EARLY INTERVENTION
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
ABCD2 Score
A
Age ≥60 = 1 pt
B
BP ≥140/90 at presentation = 1 pt
C
Clinical features
Unilateral weakness = 2 pts
Speech disturbance without weakness = 1 pt
D
Duration + Diabetes
Duration ≥60 min = 2 pts · 10–59 min = 1 pt
Diabetes = 1 pt
ABCD2 Risk Stratification
Score 0–3 (low): 2-day stroke risk ~1%. Outpatient workup acceptable if rapid-access TIA clinic available within 24 h.
Score 4–5 (moderate): 2-day stroke risk ~4%. Admit or same-day evaluation.
Score 6–7 (high): 2-day stroke risk ~8%. Admit all patients.
Rapid Workup (all TIA/minor stroke)
1
Imaging
MRI DWI within 24 h (preferred over CT)
MRA head + neck OR CTA
Carotid doppler if MRA unavailable
2
Cardiac
12-lead ECG immediately
Continuous telemetry ≥24 h
Echocardiogram (TTE or TEE)
Prolonged cardiac monitoring ≥30 days if cryptogenic
3
Labs
CBC, BMP, lipids, HbA1c
Coagulation (PT/INR, aPTT)
Hypercoagulable panel if <55 or cryptogenic
4
Early Antiplatelet
Aspirin 325 mg immediately. DAPT (aspirin + clopidogrel) × 21 days for high-risk TIA (ABCD2 ≥4) or minor ischemic stroke (NIHSS ≤3) — POINT/CHANCE trials.

Etiologic Workup — Young Stroke
1
Standard workup + extended cardiac monitoring
TEE preferred over TTE (detect PFO, aortic arch)
Prolonged Holter/loop recorder ≥30 days
MRI with DWI + vessel imaging
2
Hypercoagulable Panel
Antiphospholipid antibodies (3 tests, 12 wk apart)
Factor V Leiden · Prothrombin G20210A
Protein C/S · Antithrombin III
Homocysteine · MTHFR
3
Arteriopathy Screen
Cervical vessel imaging (dissection)
High-resolution vessel wall MRI (vasculitis)
Genetic testing: CADASIL (NOTCH3), Fabry (GLA)
PFO Management
ScenarioRecommendationEvidence
Cryptogenic stroke age 18–60, high-risk PFOPFO closure + antiplateletCLOSE, REDUCE, RESPECT trials — Class IIa
Cryptogenic stroke, low-risk PFO or >60 yrsAntiplatelet aloneRisk/benefit less clear
PFO + DVT/PE (paradoxical embolism)Anticoagulation ± closureAnticoagulate for VTE; consider closure
Cervical Artery Dissection
Most common cause of stroke in adults <45. Carotid or vertebral artery.
Classic triad: neck pain/headache + Horner syndrome + ischemic symptoms.
Treatment: antiplatelet OR anticoagulation × 3–6 months — no proven superiority of one over other (CADISS trial).
MRI with fat-sat sequences shows intramural hematoma (crescent sign).

Stroke Rehabilitation
EARLY MOBILIZATION · PT/OT/SLP · DISCHARGE PLANNING
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
Acute Rehabilitation Initiation
1
Early Mobilization
Out-of-bed activity within 24–48 h for stable patients (Class IIa). Very early mobilization (<24 h) with high-frequency, high-intensity activity is harmful — AVERT trial.
2
Swallowing Screen
Before any oral intake — validated dysphagia screen (Class I). Formal SLP evaluation if screen fails. NPO + NG tube feeding within 7 days if unable to swallow safely.
3
Therapy Intensity
PT: gait, balance, transfers — daily
OT: ADLs, upper extremity — daily
SLP: aphasia, dysphagia — daily if indicated
Target: ≥3 h/day active therapy in inpatient rehab

Discharge Disposition Criteria
Inpatient rehab (IRF): able to tolerate 3 h/day therapy; 2 disciplines needed; medically stable.
Skilled nursing facility: needs rehab but cannot tolerate IRF intensity.
Home with outpatient therapy: mild deficits, safe home environment, reliable caregiver.
mRS at discharge predicts 1-year outcome — document at every visit.

Risk Factor Management Targets
1
Blood Pressure
Target <130/80 mmHg (Class I)
Start or restart antihypertensives after 24–48 h
ACE-I + thiazide combination preferred (PROGRESS trial)
2
Lipids
High-intensity statin: atorvastatin 40–80 mg
Target LDL <70 mg/dL (non-cardioembolic)
Add ezetimibe if LDL not at goal
PCSK9 inhibitor if statin intolerant or LDL >70 on max statin
3
Diabetes
HbA1c target <7% (individualized)
GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide) reduce CV events
SGLT-2 inhibitors reduce heart failure/renal endpoints
4
Lifestyle
Smoking cessation (Class I). Moderate aerobic exercise ≥40 min, 3–4×/week. Mediterranean-style diet. Alcohol: ≤2 drinks/day men, ≤1 women.
Carotid Intervention
Symptomatic stenosis 70–99%: CEA within 2 weeks (Class I). Benefit greatest when done early.
Symptomatic stenosis 50–69%: CEA reasonable (Class IIa) — depends on patient factors and surgical risk.
Carotid stenting (CAS): reasonable alternative to CEA for symptomatic stenosis in patients at high surgical risk (Class IIa).
Asymptomatic stenosis ≥70%: CEA reasonable if perioperative risk <3% and life expectancy >5 years (Class IIa).

Status Epilepticus Protocol
1
Phase 1 — 0–5 min
Stabilize ABCs. Fingerstick glucose. IV access × 2.
2
Phase 2 — 5–20 min (Benzodiazepine)
Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV (max 4 mg)
OR Midazolam 10 mg IM (>40 kg)
OR Diazepam 0.2 mg/kg PR
3
Phase 3 — 20–40 min (2nd line)
Fosphenytoin 20 PE/kg IV
OR Valproate 40 mg/kg IV
OR Levetiracetam 60 mg/kg IV
All three are Class IIa; no single agent preferred over another.
4
Refractory (>40 min)
Intubate + midazolam/propofol/pentobarbital infusion. Continuous EEG monitoring mandatory.

Multiple Sclerosis
McDONALD 2017 CRITERIA · UPDATED DMT GUIDANCE
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
McDonald 2017 Criteria (RRMS)
DIS: ≥1 T2 lesion in ≥2 CNS areas (periventricular, cortical/juxtacortical, infratentorial, spinal).
DIT: simultaneous Gd+ and Gd− lesions, OR new T2/Gd+ on follow-up MRI, OR CSF-specific OCBs.
Diagnosis requires exclusion of alternative diagnoses.
DMT Selection Framework
1
Moderate efficacy (low-risk)
Interferons (IFN-β1a, IFN-β1b)
Glatiramer acetate
Dimethyl fumarate · Teriflunomide
2
High efficacy (escalation or early)
Natalizumab (JCV Ab monitoring)
Ocrelizumab (B-cell depletion)
Ofatumumab · Alemtuzumab · Cladribine
3
Acute Relapse
Methylprednisolone 1g IV × 3–5 days. Speeds recovery but does not change long-term outcomes.
AAN 2022
Peripheral Neuropathy
CIDP · GBS · DIABETIC NEUROPATHY
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
CIDP Diagnostic Criteria
1
Clinical Features
Progressive or relapsing-remitting symmetric limb weakness + sensory symptoms >8 weeks. Areflexia. Cranial nerve involvement possible.
2
Nerve Conduction Studies (mandatory)
Demyelination in ≥2 nerves:
Prolonged distal latency (>115% ULN)
Reduced conduction velocity (<90% LLN)
Conduction block or temporal dispersion
3
Supportive Evidence
CSF protein elevated (>45 mg/dL) without pleocytosis. MRI nerve root/plexus enhancement. Anti-NF155/CASPR1 antibodies in variants.
4
First-Line Treatment
IVIg 2 g/kg over 2–5 days, then 1 g/kg q3–4 weeks
OR Corticosteroids (prednisolone 60 mg/day)
OR Plasma exchange (5 exchanges over 2 weeks)
GBS — Acute Management
1
Admission Criteria
All GBS patients require admission. Monitor FVC q4–6 h. Intubate if FVC <15–20 mL/kg, NIF <−25 cmH₂O, or O₂ sat dropping.
2
Immunotherapy (within 2 weeks of onset)
IVIg 2 g/kg over 5 days (preferred)
OR Plasma exchange × 5 sessions
Steroids: NOT recommended (no benefit)
Diabetic Neuropathy Treatment
AgentDoseNotes
Pregabalin150–600 mg/dayFDA-approved; first-line
Duloxetine60–120 mg/dayFDA-approved; comorbid depression benefit
Gabapentin1800–3600 mg/dayTitrate slowly; renal dose adjust
Amitriptyline25–75 mg qhsAvoid in elderly (falls, anticholinergic)
⚠ Opioids in Neuropathy
AAN guidelines recommend against opioids as first- or second-line therapy for painful diabetic neuropathy due to addiction risk and modest efficacy vs. approved agents.
AAN 2023
Neuromuscular Disease
ALS · MYASTHENIA GRAVIS · MUSCULAR DYSTROPHY
⚠ Content below is summarized
Read official guideline for verbatim recommendations
Official ↗
ALS — Disease-Modifying Therapy
1
El Escorial Diagnosis
UMN + LMN signs in ≥2 regions (bulbar, cervical, thoracic, lumbosacral). EMG for LMN evidence. MRI to exclude structural mimics.
2
Neuroprotective Agents
Riluzole 50 mg BID (extends survival ~3 mo)
Edaravone IV (functional decline ↓ in select pts)
AMX0035 (sodium phenylbutyrate/tauroursodeoxycholic acid)
3
Respiratory Monitoring
FVC every 3 months. NIV when FVC <50% or symptomatic orthopnea. PEG before FVC falls <50%.
Myasthenia Gravis — Management
1
Diagnosis
AChR Ab (85% sensitivity in generalized MG)
MuSK Ab (if AChR negative, 40–70%)
SFEMG: most sensitive (>95%)
Edrophonium test: limited use now
2
Treatment Ladder
Pyridostigmine 30–60 mg q4–6 h (symptomatic)
Prednisone (start low, titrate up)
Azathioprine / mycophenolate (steroid-sparing)
Complement inhibitors: eculizumab, ravulizumab
3
Myasthenic Crisis
ICU admission. IVIg 2 g/kg OR plasma exchange. Avoid succinylcholine (prolonged block). Hold pyridostigmine acutely (↑ secretions).
MG: Drugs to Avoid
Fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides — worsen NMJ transmission.
Magnesium infusions — can precipitate crisis.
Beta-blockers, procainamide — use with caution.
Thymectomy: recommended in AChR+ generalized MG, especially age <65.

Alzheimer's Disease — Workup
1
Cognitive Screening
MoCA (<26 = impaired) — preferred
MMSE (<24 = impaired)
CDR for staging (0=normal, 0.5=MCI, 1–3=dementia)
2

3

Donanemab: FDA approved 2024
Require APOE4 genotyping (↑ ARIA risk)

WHO 2021 CNS Tumor Classification
1
Glioma Grading (integrated diagnosis)
Astrocytoma IDH-mutant: Grade 2–4
Oligodendroglioma IDH-mut/1p19q-codelet: Grade 2–3
Glioblastoma IDH-wildtype: Grade 4 (always)
H3 K27M-altered diffuse glioma: Grade 4
2
Key Molecular Markers
IDH1/2 mutation: prognostic (better outcome)
MGMT promoter methylation: predicts TMZ response
TERT promoter: GBM marker
EGFR amplification: GBM feature
GBM Standard of Care (Stupp Protocol)
1
Surgery
Maximal safe resection. 5-ALA fluorescence guidance to maximize extent of resection (Class I evidence).
2
Concurrent chemoradiation
RT: 60 Gy in 30 fractions over 6 weeks
+ Temozolomide 75 mg/m² daily during RT
3
Adjuvant chemotherapy
Temozolomide 150–200 mg/m² × 5 days, q28d × 6 cycles
+ Tumor Treating Fields (TTFields) if MGMT-methylated
Brain Metastases
SituationPreferred ApproachNotes
1–3 mets, good KPSSRS ± surgeryAvoid WBRT if possible (cognitive SE)
4–10 mets, smallSRSHippocampal-avoidance WBRT if SRS not feasible

Seizure Prophylaxis in Brain Tumors
Do NOT use prophylactic AEDs in seizure-naïve brain tumor patients (AAN Class I recommendation).
If seizures occur, prefer levetiracetam (no CYP450 interaction with chemotherapy).
Avoid phenytoin/phenobarbital — reduce temozolomide and other chemotherapy levels.
